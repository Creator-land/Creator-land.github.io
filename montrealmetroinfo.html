Montreal Metro

Basic Info

The Montreal Metro is Montreal's major rapid transit network. Spanning 69.2 km split across four lines, it has 68 stations and is the second-busiest rapid transit system in North America, behind only New York and Mexico City. The Metro's lines are the Green, Orange, Blue, and Yellow lines, and it interchanges with the REM at Bonaventure. It will interchange with the REM in the future at McGill and Edouard-Montpetit stations. The centre of the metro network is not in downtown, but at the busy transfer station of Berri-UQAM, where the Green, Orange, and Yellow lines come together. (It is also my least favourite station on the network, the transfer to the Yellow line SUCKS.) The Metro utilises mainly Bombardier-Alstom MPM-10s (nicknamed "Azur") with some Bombardier MR-73s.

Orange Line

The Orange Line is the longest line in the system. It is 30km long with 31 stations, from Cote-Vertu to Montmorency. It was opened on October 14, 1966, and last expanded in 2007. It only uses the MPM-10.
