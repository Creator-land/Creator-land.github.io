Montreal REM

Basic Info

The Montreal REM is a partially under construction light metro line serving the Montreal area. It currently contains 5 stations and 16.6km of track, from Brossard to Gare Centrale, with 21 more stations and 50.4km more of track under construction. Unlike many Canadian transit projects, the REM is not managed municipally or even (directly) provincially, but is a project of CDPQ infra. The REM operates Alstom Metropolis Light Metro Vehicles, and is fully automated. The mainline will run from Bois-Franc to Brossard, and three branches will run into it, one to Deux-Montagnes, one to Sainte-Anne-De-Bellevue, and one to Aeroport Montreal-Trudeau (Montreal's Airport). All are in the North of the system.
