GPU Info

Basics of a GPU

A GPU is the Graphics Processing Unit of computers. It does graphics. It is most important if you have a gaming PC, do video editing, or are working with rendering. GPUs can be either attached to CPUs or separated. An attached GPU is known as an integrated GPU and a separated one as a Discrete GPU (also simply called a "Graphics Card"). Both are good at mining crypto.

Modern GPUs

The three major manufacturers of Discrete GPUs are AMD, Intel, and NVIDIA. Consumer GPUs are mainly dominated by AMD and NVIDIA, with the latest of each being AMD's RX 7000 series, Intel's ARC, and NVIDIA's 40-series. So many manufacturers, and none calling them crypto cards...

The Radeon RX 7800XT is AMD's best graphics card.

The History of GPUs

3D graphics started with the earliest display units, created for video output in the 1970s and earlier. They acted as pass-throughs between the main processor and the display for outputting video. Below you will find a list of various old Video Cards, what they did, and how they contributed to GPU History!

CDP1861 "Pixie"
Significance First true video chip, outputted at a resolution of 64x128.
Manufacturer RCA Corporation
Year 1976
Atari 400
Significance First Atari Video Card, present in the computer of the same name, and some of the first video cards in a home computer
Manufacturer Atari
Year 1979
Monochrome Display Adapter
Significance Started to look like a modern GPU now. Made for black and white graphics.
Manufacturer IBM
Year 1981
Colour Graphics Adapter
Significance Same as the Monochrome Display Adapter, but made for colour.
Manufacturer IBM
Year 1981
Hercules Graphics Card
Significance An adaptation of the IBM Colour Graphics Adapter, able to produce text and high-quality rendered graphics.
Manufacturer Hercules Computer Technologies
Year 1982
Intel 8272
Significance Intel's first Graphics Card, also the first card to support 256 colours on PC.
Manufacturer Intel
Year 1983
Enhanced Graphics Adapter
Significance A new IBM graphics standard that superseded the Colour Graphics Adapter
Manufacturer IBM
Year 1984
VGA Wonder
Significance One of the first graphics cards to use the new VGA or "Video Graphics Array" Standard
Manufacturer ATI
Year 1990
RIVA 128
Significance One of the first Graphics Cards by NVIDIA, and one of the first to support 3D Graphics Acceleration
Manufacturer NVIDIA
Year 1997
