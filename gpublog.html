GPUs

Basic Info

What does a GPU do?

A GPU is the Graphics Processing unit of the computers. It does graphics. It is most important if you have a gaming PC or are working in rendering. GPUs can be either attached to CPUs or separated. An attached GPU is known as an integrated GPU and a separated one as a Discrete GPU (also simply called a "Graphics Card"). Both are good at mining crypto.

Who makes GPUs?

The three major manufacturers of Discrete GPUs are AMD, Intel, and NVIDIA. Consumer GPUs are mainly dominated by AMD and NVIDIA, with the latest of each being AMD's RX 7000 series, Intel's ARC, and NVIDIA's 40-series. So many manufacturers, and none calling them crypto cards...

History of GPUs

Origins

3D graphics started with the earliest display units, created for video output in the 1970s and earlier. They acted as pass-throughs between the main processor and the display for outputting video. These were followed by the first dedicated video chip from the smaller parts of processors before them the "Pixie" video chip or the CDP1861 by RCA, and it was capable of outputting NTSC compatible video signal at 62x128. It was created in 1976.
